Data Brokers and Opting Out

26.05.2021 daily data privacy

There’s a lot of money being made from my data. Well not specifically my data, but our collective data. Just take a look at Zuckerberg’s net worth.

It’s not just Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, or TikTok that sell our data however. My personal data is bought and sold by dozens, or possibly hundreds of smaller companies. I wanted to know who these companies were, and stumbled on this article.

A Vermont law requires data brokers to register with the state. There’s a lot registered unfortunately.

Opting Out

These companies are required to let you opt out of the collection and sale of your data. Here’s a convenient GitHub repo with instruction stopping data collection from brokers.

The biggest caveat is that most of the opt-out actions require writing and sending emails or even physical letters. I hope to see this becoming all automated in the future.
